Chiang Kai-shek, also known as Chiang Chung-cheng, Chiang Chieh-shih, Cheung Kai-shek, Jiang Zhongzheng, and Jiang Jieshi, was a Chinese Nationalist politician, revolutionary and military leader, who served as the leader of the Republic of China (ROC) from 1928 to until his death in 1975. From 1928 until 1949 he served as leader of the ROC in mainland China, and from 1949 until 1975, as leader of the ROC in Taiwan.
