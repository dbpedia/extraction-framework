The pronunciation of GIF, an acronym for the Graphics Interchange Format, has been disputed since the 1990s. Popularly pronounced in English as a one-syllable word, the acronym's most common pronunciations are and, differing in the phoneme represented by the letter G. Many public figures and institutions have taken sides in the debate; Steve Wilhite, the creator of the format, gave a speech at the 2013 Webby Awards arguing that the soft g pronunciation is correct. Others have pointed to the term's origin from abbreviation of the hard-g word graphics to argue for the other pronunciation.
